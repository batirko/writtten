# Snapshot: Due-Diligence Audit (2026-06-10)

> A five-lens external-style audit (product strategy, business/OSS, design & UX, architecture, applied AI/eval) conducted against the full doc corpus: `CLAUDE.md`, `docs/concept.md`, `docs/product-requirements.md`, `docs/features.md`, `docs/architecture.md`, `docs/plan.md`, plus sampled projects (`quality_remediation_synthesis`, `philosophy_guardrails`, `emotional_register`, `evaluator_quality_ratchet`, `prompt_quality_observations`) and both prior snapshots. The brief: for every risk the docs already name, judge whether it is mitigated or merely acknowledged; then find the problems the docs are not seeing. Calibrated against the project's own stated goals (OSS pet project; success = "a tool people want to use and contribute to"), not a commercial rubric.

## Verdict

Worth continuing — as a research artifact and a craft project, it's already one of the best-documented small codebases we've audited, and the philosophy-as-CI instinct is genuinely novel. But it is currently a product with **zero users and a five-phase plan that never schedules one**. Every field observation in the repo (OBS-001 through OBS-023, both snapshots) comes from the founder pasting test documents at their own tool. The single biggest risk is not the drafting-annex ceiling, the free-tier quality cliff, or flattery-learning — the docs name all of those. It's that the project has built an exquisite apparatus for _holding_ the inversion before producing any evidence that anyone _wants_ it held, and the one piece of register-discipline field data that exists (OBS-010) shows the founder themselves — the most philosophically bought-in user on Earth — asking for the prescription the product is defined by withholding. Condition for continuing: before any Phase 5 polish work, put it in front of five external PMs with their real drafts, and instrument how often the hero moment fires un-planted. Everything else is downstream of that.

---

## Prioritized findings

### 1. [FATAL] The central bet is unfalsified at n=0, and no phase schedules falsification.

**Lens:** Plan & scope / Thesis. **Evidence:** `docs/plan.md` Phases 0–6 contain no milestone of the form "external user writes a real document." Both snapshots (2026-06-03, 2026-06-04) are self-administered paste tests on `phase1-test-text.md`. Phase 5's open items are visual style, onboarding, emotional register — all polish _for_ users who don't yet exist. **Why it matters:** The fidelity bar, the discomfort budget, the persona spec, the dismissal-learning taxonomy — these are all theories of other people's psychology, tuned against the one person guaranteed to agree with them. The docs explicitly call the silence-during-drafting rhythm "a bet, not a settled truth" (`docs/product-requirements.md` §3) but the plan never places the bet on a table where it can lose. This is the same drift the 2026-06-04 snapshot caught at the roadmap level ("the easy axis crowds out the hard axis") happening one level higher: measurable internal quality work crowding out unmeasurable external validation. **What would change my mind:** Session notes from even three external PMs using it on their own in-flight docs. Not a demo — a draft they cared about.

### 2. [FATAL] The hero capability has an unmeasured base rate, and the instrumentation is structurally blind to its failures.

**Lens:** Thesis / Applied AI. **Evidence:** `docs/concept.md` names contradiction-at-distance the hero ("the whole v0 demo should be built to land this single moment"); R4.6 calls it "the load-bearing wow." Every recorded catch is of a _planted_ contradiction (the Q2/Q3 fixture — 2026-06-03 snapshot: "successfully identified a planted contradiction"). No doc anywhere estimates how often a real PRD contains a genuine logical contradiction the author wouldn't self-catch. Meanwhile the observation-log process can only capture false _positives_ — a user never reports the contradiction the tool missed, because they don't know it's there. And the Jaccard top-10 lexical prefilter (`docs/architecture.md`, Phase 3 prefilter decision) will silently drop semantically-related-but-lexically-distant claim pairs ("Q2" vs. "the second quarter"; "20% lift" vs. "one in five") — eroding hero-recall in exactly the invisible way. **Why it matters:** If genuine contradiction-at-distance fires, say, once per several documents, the _daily_ experience of the product is the supporting cast — `clarity`, `undefined_jargon`, `unsupported_claim` — which the field log shows is the noisiest, most trust-eroding surface (OBS-001/003/005/019/022/023 are all fast-tier false positives). A product whose wow is rare and whose routine is its weakest output has a retention shape the docs never confront: there is no first-week retention story anywhere in the corpus. **What would change my mind:** A corpus study — run the ledger pipeline over 15–20 real PRDs (the research gate already specified for the Phase 6 taxonomy expansion in `docs/projects/observation_taxonomy_and_priority.md`) and count un-planted true contradictions. That study is cheap and is currently scheduled _behind_ the feature it should gate.

### 3. [MAJOR] The free tier cannot deliver the product's own bar; BYO-key is effectively mandatory, and the docs treat this as a gradient when it's a cliff.

**Lens:** Applied AI / Business-OSS. **Evidence:** The harness docs (`CLAUDE.md`) state the binding free-tier constraint is ~20 requests/day per Flash model and **zero** RPD for `gemini-2.5-pro` — the model the strong tier is designed around. `docs/projects/evaluation_signal_quality.md` records the consequence: "free-tier 'strong' checks run on a weak model and emit confident false contradictions" — a false hero-observation, the precise failure R4.4 says costs the entire feed ("one 'contradiction' that isn't one and the user discounts the feed"). OBS-019 shows flash-lite flagging a metric _despite the exact negative example being verbatim in the prompt_; OBS-023 shows it ignoring the provided document-context field. Yet Phase 3's exit criterion — "free tier works with no key and acceptable latency/cost" — is marked done. **Why it matters:** The adoption funnel for an OSS tool is the free first-run. If the free first-run either exhausts its quota mid-session or emits confidently wrong contradictions, the trust asymmetry the requirements doc describes executes against the product on day one. The honest framing is "this product requires a paid Gemini key," which shrinks the persona to PMs who hold personal API keys — a very thin slice — and that constraint should be a stated, examined decision, not an emergent one. (`docs/features.md` still describes the free tier as "enough to demonstrate the loop.") **What would change my mind:** Tier-2 live scorecards showing the free-tier pool clearing the precision floor on the fixture corpus, plus a session trace showing a full real PRD revision fitting inside free RPD limits.

### 4. [MAJOR] The actual usage pattern that emerged is paste-an-existing-draft — which collapses the product toward the one-shot audit it defines itself against, and makes the drafting-annex risk worse than acknowledged.

**Lens:** Product & UX / Thesis. **Evidence:** The tooling's own evolution is the tell: `docs/projects/bulk_paste_evaluation.md` and Markdown import became Phase 4 core work; both snapshots are paste tests; `docs/features.md` insists it's "not a one-shot audit," but nothing in the field record shows sustained organic _writing_ in the tool. `docs/concept.md` acknowledges the drafting-annex ceiling as a _long-term stickiness_ problem — but for the PM persona it's a _per-session_ friction problem: PMs draft inside Notion/Google Docs/Confluence where their comments, templates, and collaborators already live. There may be no discrete "drafting moment" to own; drafting and destination are the same surface for this persona. **Why it matters:** If the dominant real behavior is "paste finished draft → read observations → leave," then the settling detection, temporal rhythm, warm-up curve, and lifecycle machinery — the majority of R3 and R5, and a large share of total engineering effort — service a loop users don't inhabit. What remains is "LLM document reviewer with excellent span anchoring," which a Notion AI or Docs-Gemini sidebar can approximate in a quarter. The defensible part of the thesis is the _ambient writing companion_; the version that survives paste-first usage is the commodity part. **What would change my mind:** Telemetry-free but checkable evidence (session notes, harness event streams) of users _typing_ documents in the editor over multiple sessions, with the rhythm machinery visibly earning its keep.

### 5. [MAJOR] The privacy story is over-claimed: on the free tier it's arguably worse than the tools it positions against.

**Lens:** Architecture / Positioning. **Evidence:** `docs/architecture.md` admits document content goes to the model provider and frames the future local-model adapter as "the payoff that turns local-first storage into genuine no-egress privacy" — so the gap is acknowledged. What's not confronted: Google's free-tier Gemini API terms permit training on submitted content. The first persona's core artifact is the _confidential PRD_ — unreleased strategy, metrics, fraud rules (the project's own test fixture!). **Why it matters:** "Local-first and privacy-respecting" (Invariant 5) is currently a statement about _storage_, while every settled block of a confidential document is shipped to a third party that may train on it. An enterprise PM who reads the architecture honestly cannot use the free tier at all, which compounds finding 3. Acknowledged ≠ mitigated; here the marketing copy ("sensitive PRDs never need to leave the machine") outruns the system's behavior. **What would change my mind:** Either the Ollama adapter landing earlier than Phase 6, or the privacy section rewritten to state plainly what leaves the machine, when, under which provider terms — with the free-tier training-data issue named.

### 6. [MAJOR] The only register-discipline field datum contradicts the register, and the adopted answer ("severity ∝ maturity") is a hypothesis promoted to a conclusion without a test.

**Lens:** Product & UX. **Evidence:** OBS-010 (`docs/logs/prompt_quality_observations.md`): the user (the founder) received a correct, register-compliant `missing_topic` observation and _wanted_ "a softer suggestion to 'think about adding a section about potential risks'" — i.e., the prescription. `docs/projects/quality_remediation_synthesis.md` R2 resolves this elegantly: the abrasiveness was about _when_, not _what_, so fix timing and the register holds. That's a genuinely good hypothesis. It is also exactly the move the requirements doc warns about — a reasonable-sounding reframe that lets the project avoid confronting whether "locate, don't prescribe" survives contact with a tired user at 5pm. The R2c design spec (smart-feed vs. manual control) is still 🟠 unstarted. **Why it matters:** If maturity-aware severity _doesn't_ dissolve the discomfort, the adoption ceiling is real and structural — and that's worth knowing before the Phase 5 emotional-register investment. Right now the project has one data point on its hardest question and has explained it away. **What would change my mind:** External users receiving correctly-timed, register-compliant critique on mature drafts and reporting it as respectful rather than cold. That's the experiment; OBS-010's hypothesis makes it falsifiable, which is to the project's credit.

### 7. [MAJOR] The quantitative eval bar is far below the prose bar, and the ground truth is self-authored.

**Lens:** Applied AI. **Evidence:** `docs/product-requirements.md` R4.4: "one 'contradiction' that isn't one and the user discounts the entire feed" — an implicit precision bar near 1.0 for high-severity types. `docs/projects/evaluator_quality_ratchet.md`: Tier-2 live floor is precision ≥ 0.7, recall ≥ 0.8, over ~6–8 fixtures, all authored and labeled by the prompt author, with the hero check's mock-mode determinism still a known gap (`CLAUDE.md`). Tier 1's precision=1.0 is replay of frozen responses — it guards the pipeline, not the model. **Why it matters:** A 0.7 precision floor permits the feed to be 30% wrong and still pass CI — three real issues _and_ two pieces of noise, the exact ratio §4 rejects. And n≈7 fixtures gives the floor no statistical meaning; a single flaky fixture swings it 14 points. The ratchet is real infrastructure (credit below), but calling Phase 4's "regression suite guards the bar" exit criterion met is generous: it guards _a_ bar, well below _the_ bar. **What would change my mind:** A corpus at the 20–40-doc scale the project already targets, with at least some labels from a second rater, and per-type precision floors that reflect the trust asymmetry (contradiction ≥ 0.95, nits can be looser).

### 8. [MINOR] The anti-taxonomy's "structural" enforcement is weaker than claimed — `clarity` is a laundering slot.

**Lens:** Applied AI. **Evidence:** `docs/features.md` claims surface nits have "no slot to map to." But "ambiguous, vague, or hard-to-parse passage" (`clarity`) is precisely where a sentence-length or word-choice nit dresses itself up — "this sentence is hard to parse" is a readability complaint wearing a meaning costume. Enforcement beyond the slot argument is one prompt instruction plus one fixture, aimed at a model OBS-019 proved ignores verbatim negative examples. **Why it matters:** The surface-drift gravity well (tension #2) will arrive _through_ `clarity`, not around the taxonomy, and the current fixture (one surface-flawed doc) won't reliably catch it. **What would change my mind:** A per-type discrimination fixture set — surface-flawed-but-clear passages that `clarity` must not flag — and field samples of `clarity` output audited against the anti-taxonomy.

### 9. [MINOR] The OSS half of the success metric is wholly unserved through five phases.

**Lens:** Business / OSS. **Evidence:** The stated success metric is "a tool people want to use and **contribute to**" (`docs/concept.md`). The project has no name ("Sidecar (placeholder)"), the extension API is Phase 6, the model router in practice supports exactly one provider (every resilience mechanism — rotation pools, RPD budgets, cool-downs — is Gemini-shaped; non-Gemini adapters sit in "Discovered/unscheduled"), and nothing in the repo addresses contributor onboarding. **Why it matters:** Non-monetization is a defensible stance for a pet project. But an OSS project's substitute for revenue momentum is community momentum, and there is none and no plan for any. If the OSS framing is real, it needs the same scheduling discipline everything else here gets; if it's decorative, drop it from the success metric so the project is honest with itself about being a solo artifact. **What would change my mind:** A name, a README that pitches the inversion, and one external contributor PR on any of the three seams.

### 10. [MINOR] Shipped-phase bookkeeping is ahead of reality in two trust-relevant spots.

**Lens:** Plan & scope. **Evidence:** R5.2 ("resolution reflects the problem being gone") is 🟡 in `docs/acceptance-testing/fidelity-criteria.md` — "cosmetic span-drop can still close" — inside a Phase 2 marked fully implemented; per-user dismissal learning is described in `docs/features.md` while the app is single-document (`DOC_ID` constant), so the per-user surface doesn't exist. **Why it matters:** Small, but this is a project whose entire method is honest self-assessment; the green checkmarks should inherit that standard. **What would change my mind:** Nothing needed — just reconcile the markers.

## What's genuinely strong

1. **The observation-log → synthesis → remediation loop.** `docs/projects/quality_remediation_synthesis.md` collapsing 32 raw field observations into six root causes — and correctly identifying that most "prompt problems" were trigger/reconciliation/anchoring bugs — is senior-grade engineering judgment. Most teams never build this muscle at any size.
2. **Philosophy enforced as code, not prose.** No-injection as a structural property with lint enforcement, kind/severity-aware suppression as a _data-model_ guard against flattery-learning (G1), anti-taxonomy as CI fixtures — the instinct that "be deep is not a constraint a model self-enforces" and must be gated mechanically is correct and rare.
3. **The `strategic_tension` split** is textbook honest classification: field data showed a misfire class, the fix was a new typed bucket with a softer register rather than a loosened prompt. That's the taxonomy discipline working as designed.
4. **The claim ledger + incremental eval architecture** is the right shape: it makes cross-document checking affordable by construction, and the section-as-eval-unit correction shows debugging at the right level of abstraction. ProseMirror-for-position-mapping is the correct editor bet.
5. **The requirements doc itself.** Tiering by _fidelity_ rather than completeness, and naming "the threats arrive as the model trying to be helpful," is the sharpest articulation of an AI-product philosophy we've seen at this scale. Don't break it — test it.

## The 3 things to do next

1. **Run the base-rate study before anything else.** Take 15–20 real PRDs (the founder's, colleagues', public post-mortems), run the pipeline, and count: un-planted true contradictions found, per-type precision in the wild, free-tier vs. paid-tier delta. One week of work. It directly de-risks findings 2, 3, and 7, and this exact study is already specified as the Phase 6 taxonomy gate — move it to now.
2. **Put it in front of five external PMs with their real drafts** — before visual style, before onboarding, before emotional register. Watch whether they write or paste, whether located-critique-without-a-fix lands as respect or coldness on a mature draft (the OBS-010 hypothesis), and whether anything brings them back for a second session. This is the cheapest possible falsification of the central bet, and every Phase 5 item will be better-aimed after it.
3. **Reconcile the free-tier story.** Decide explicitly: is the free tier a real tier or a demo? If BYO-key is effectively mandatory for the product to meet its own bar, say so in `docs/concept.md`, redesign the first-run around it, and name the free-tier privacy terms honestly. The current ambiguity quietly undermines trust, adoption, and the "local-first" positioning all at once.

---

## Closing note

The docs' self-awareness is this project's greatest asset and its subtlest trap. Naming a risk in beautiful prose feels like handling it. Of the five risks the docs name, only flattery-learning is genuinely _mitigated_ (G1, structurally); the annex ceiling, the silence bet, the egress gap, and the no-ground-truth problem are acknowledged and still fully live. The next phase of this project should produce fewer immaculate documents and more contact with people who haven't read any of them.
