# Competitor Snapshot — Proof (proofeditor.ai)

*Date: 2026-06-13. Source: website, founder essay, open-source SDK, and hands-on UX session against the live app.*

## What it is

**Proof** is an **agent-first collaborative document editor** — "Google Docs rebuilt so AI agents are first-class authors." Not a proofreader, despite the domain name.

- **Maker:** Dan Shipper / Every Inc. **Launched March 2026.** Free, no login, **open-source** ([EveryInc/proof-sdk](https://github.com/EveryInc/proof-sdk)).
- **Thesis (founder):** *"Most of the writing that will happen in the next decade will be generated by AI agents"* — so agents deserve a proper collaborative tool instead of markdown files stuck "in 1999."
- **Target docs:** bug reports, PRDs, implementation plans, research briefs, copy audits, strategy memos, proposals — overlaps heavily with our PM persona.

## Mechanics (verified hands-on)

- **Provenance rail** — colored gutter in the left margin. Green = human, purple = AI. Implemented as **per-character DOM spans** ("every character knows its author"; confirmed in the DOM).
- **Suggestions** — AI edits land as **track-changes** (green underline = insert, red strikethrough = delete); user **accepts or rejects** each. A "Suggesting" toggle lets humans write in suggestion-mode too.
- **Comments** — agents comment on a span to explain reasoning / ask questions; threaded human replies.
- **AX (agent experience)** — the novel part. "Add agent" emits a prompt with a **tokenized doc URL + HTTP bridge**: `/presence`, `/state`, `/events/stream` (SSE), edit APIs, and a `proof.SKILL.md`. External agents (Claude Code, Codex) *join the doc as participants*, watch an event stream, and write back.
- **Original prototype framing (worth coveting):** mark which AI-touched passages you've **reviewed, and how closely** — a *trust / review-depth* layer on AI text.

## Reception

Thin and early. Almost entirely founder + Every-team amplification on X, internal-dogfooding anecdotes, a "replaces Google Docs" video. **No substantial independent or critical reviews found.** Treat "well-received" as unproven.

## Relationship to us — opposite ends of one axis

Proof and Sidecar answer the same question — *"what is AI's role relative to the user's text?"* — with opposite bets:

| | **Proof** | **Sidecar** |
|---|---|---|
| AI's job | Co-author — writes, edits, suggests | Provoker — observes, never writes |
| Primary affordance | **Accept / reject** suggestions | *Forbidden* (Hard Invariant #1) |
| Problem solved | AI writing is trapped & unattributed | AI writing makes humans stop thinking |

**Proof's core feature is the exact "Apply suggestion" button [CLAUDE.md](../../CLAUDE.md) forbids by name.** They built the thing our product exists to refuse. This makes them the cleanest possible foil — we occupy the negative space they leave behind.

## What it validates

1. **"AI on the side, human in control" is now a category, not a quirk** — a second well-resourced team converged on a sidecar-shaped, margin-rail UI. They fill the rail with edits; we fill it with observations.
2. **Provenance/authorship anxiety is real** — Proof's whole reason to exist is "who wrote what." Our answer to "is this mine?" is structurally stronger: with Sidecar it's always 100% the user's.
3. **TipTap/ProseMirror substrate confirmed** — their per-character provenance needed the same decoration + position-mapping machinery our anchoring depends on.

## What it should prompt us to rethink

1. **Trust / review-depth (Phase 5 "Trust" milestone).** Their "have you actually reviewed this, and how closely" idea is **principle-compatible** (it's about human engagement, not AI editing). Pull it forward as design inspiration: a richer "I've grappled with this" state beyond binary dismiss/keep.
2. **Provenance, inverted, as a Sidecar primitive.** They track *which characters AI wrote*. We could track *which passages the user revised after an observation* — a "you thought about this" rail that **celebrates** human authorship instead of flagging AI's. Same idiom, opposite semantics; a feature only our product can honestly ship.
3. **Decide on external-agent collaboration ("AX") explicitly.** Proof exposes an HTTP bridge so outside agents collaborate in the doc. We should make this a *decided* non-goal, not an unconsidered gap. → added to `docs/concept.md` non-goals this session.
4. **Name Proof as the cautionary example in `docs/projects/philosophy_guardrails.md`** — "this is the product we become if we add one accept/reject affordance." Watching it live, the pull toward "just let it fix this" is palpable.

## Principle-safe borrows

- **No-login, link-first, ~2-second first-doc onboarding** — a real friction bar our local-first PWA can match.
- **Reply-to-observation** — letting the user annotate their *own* reasoning against a provocation ("addressed in §3") is principle-safe and feeds `dismissal_should_teach`. Difference from Proof: their thread negotiates with an author; ours records the user's own thinking.

## Do NOT copy

Accept/reject + AI-authored insertions. Their core; our anti-pattern.
