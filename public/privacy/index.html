Privacy & Terms

How writtten handles your writing, in plain language. Last updated 15 July 2026

The short version

writtten runs in your browser. Your document and everything the tool derives from it are stored only on your device — there are no accounts and no writtten server holding your work. The one time your writing leaves your device is when it’s sent to the AI provider you chose, using your own API key, to generate observations. writtten sets no analytics and no tracking cookies.

Where your writing lives

Your document, the claim ledger, block summaries, observations, and your settings are stored locally in your browser (IndexedDB). There are no accounts and no required backend, and none of it is copied to a writtten server. Clear your browser’s data for this site and it’s gone. That’s what “local-first” means here.

When your writing leaves your device

Evaluation is not local. When a block of text settles, that block’s text and the claims extracted from it are sent to the AI model provider you configured — Google Gemini, OpenAI, or Anthropic — directly from your browser, using your own API key. It never passes through a writtten server. What the provider does with it is governed by that provider’s terms, not by writtten.

So, honestly: for any document you actually evaluate, your text is seen by a third-party model provider. “Local-first” describes where your work is stored, not whether it’s ever sent anywhere.

The free Gemini tier is the sharp edge. If you use a free Google Gemini key, Google’s free-tier API terms may permit them to use the text you submit to improve their models. Don’t put anything you consider confidential into the free tier.

For confidential work, use a paid key — or a provider whose terms exclude training on your data. See Google’s Gemini API terms.

Your API keys

Any API key you add is stored only in this browser, in localStorage, in plain text — the honest trade-off for a tool with no backend to hold secrets for you. Your key is sent only to the provider it belongs to, and never to writtten.

Analytics and tracking

writtten collects nothing about you. There is no analytics, no telemetry, no tracking cookies, and no third-party trackers in the app. The site is served as static files from Cloudflare Pages; like any web host, the host may keep standard request logs (such as IP addresses) at its edge — writtten itself neither collects nor has access to any per-visitor data.
